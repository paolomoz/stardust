rules

  • Display headlines at weight 300 — the brand leans light, not bold, on the hero.
  • Primary CTA label is always Space Mono; secondary/ghost buttons in Space Grotesk.
  • Body softens to ~0.85 opacity on dark surfaces. Full opacity on cream.
  • Marker accent (Permanent Marker) is reserved — one scrawl per section, max.

// motifs

The signatures.

Strike-through rewrite

Headline words crossed out and replaced inline — "ice cream AI agent". The crossed phrase reads first; the real one lands second.

Hero headlines only. Once per page.

Ice-cream-meets-terminal

Dev-shop copy smuggled in through dessert metaphors — "the self-licking ice cream cone", "Under the waffle hood", "chmod +x everything".

Section headers and eyebrows. Don't force it.

Dark / cream alternation

Long pages alternate dark Cone Black sections with bright Ice Cream Cream sections. The rhythm is part of the identity.

Dark for atmosphere + demos, cream for feature walkthroughs and install.

Tool-logo wall

Grid of recognizable product logos (GitHub, Figma, Slack, Notion, …) as a "works with everything" signal.

Monochrome only. Never colored logos.

Low-opacity body ink

Hero subhead renders at ~0.85 on #F0F0F0 — soft, not pure white on near-black.

Lede copy on dark surfaces. Full opacity on cream.

// photography

No humans. No hands on keyboards. The product is the photograph.

Minimal, product-forward screenshots and silent-video demos. When imagery is needed, it's the UI itself — terminal, browser, app chrome — framed clean on Cone Black or Ice Cream Cream.

do

  • Lead with product video or UI capture, not a photograph.
  • Crop tight to the action — no decorative chrome unless it tells the story.
  • 2× renders for crispness; subtle 8px rounding on frames.

don't

  • No stock "diverse team at laptop" photography.
  • No humans, hands on keyboards, or office interiors.
  • No gradient backdrops — flat brand surfaces only.

// voice

A dev tool with a sugar high.

Technically precise, winkingly self-aware, allergic to enterprise-speak. Says what it does, then makes a joke about saying what it does.

Playful Technical Self-aware Concrete Deadpan Corporate Hype-speak Hedged Earnest-SaaS Overpromising

do

An ice-cream AI agent that lives in your browser.

Strike-through rewrite lands the joke and the pitch at once.

SLICC — the self-licking ice cream cone.

The tagline is the joke. No explanation offered.

Under the waffle hood.

Section header for "how it works".

chmod +x everything.

Three words, one idea, zero fluff.

Your browser sessions, its hands.

Mechanical metaphor in six words.

No server, no API keys, nothing to approve.

Rhythmic triplet. Each clause shorter. Ends on the zinger.

don't

Revolutionize your workflow with next-generation agentic AI.

Every word is a buzzword. SLICC would never.

SLICC empowers enterprise teams to unlock productivity.

"Empowers" and "unlock" are on the permanent banlist.

We're excited to announce…

The brand is deadpan. It's not excited; it's busy.

An intelligent automation platform for the modern browser.

Generic category copy. No joke, no specifics, no voice.

hard rules

  • No exclamation points. The brand is dry.
  • No "empower", "unlock", "revolutionize", "seamless", "cutting-edge".
  • Earn every dessert metaphor — don't sprinkle them on every heading.
  • Short clauses. Rhythmic triplets are on-brand.
  • If a line could appear on any other SaaS homepage, rewrite it.

// tone

Deadpan by default. Jokes per screen is the only dial.

To Disarm

Make a very technical product feel approachable with one small joke per screen — never two.

To Clarify

Explain what SLICC does in concrete mechanical terms: "your browser sessions, its hands."

To Respect the reader

Assume a developer. Skip the setup; land the point.

Clever

Hero headlines, section eyebrows, taglines. One gag per surface, then move on.

Clear

Install steps, "bring your own tokens", any copy answering "will this break my machine?". Jokes off.
