# Intent
Make visitors feel like they've found something they didn't know they were looking for. Break the taboo immediately with warmth and wit. Show that planning your death can be the most alive thing you do.

# Audience
Adults 35–70 who've thought about death but haven't done anything about it. They arrive curious — maybe from social media, a friend's recommendation, or a press feature. They're open-minded but might still feel a little weird about it.

# Key Messages
1. Death deserves better than beige carpet and organ music — and so do you.
2. We've helped 200+ people throw the party of a lifetime. Their lifetime.
3. This isn't morbid. This is love with a playlist.

# Calls to Action
- Primary: "Start Planning"
- Secondary: "Explore Our Services"

# Tone
Maximum personality. This is where we lead with irreverence and warmth. Bold headlines, short punchy copy, emotional resonance. The first impression should feel like a neon sign flickering on in a dark room.
