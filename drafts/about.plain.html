It Started with an Oven

In 2018, in a backyard in Bushwick, we built our first wood-fired oven out of salvaged brick and iron. We weren't chefs. We were just two people who'd eaten real pizza in Naples and couldn't find anything like it back home.

We spent a year getting the dough wrong. Then one night, everything clicked — the fermentation, the heat, the timing. Sixty seconds. Charred edges, soft center, that specific leopard-spotted underside. We knew we had something.

Ferro & Fiamma opened its doors in 2020. The oven is bigger now. The mission hasn't changed.

The Dough

72 hours of cold fermentation. No shortcuts. The dough develops flavor, structure, and digestibility that you can't rush. Every batch is mixed by hand and given the time it needs.

The Source

San Marzano DOP tomatoes from Campania. Fior di latte from a Brooklyn creamery we visit every week. Caputo 00 flour from Naples. We know every supplier by name.

The Fire

Our oven burns oak and cherry wood to 900°F. Every pizza cooks in sixty seconds. The heat creates the char, the bubble, the flavor that defines real Neapolitan pizza.
