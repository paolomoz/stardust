# Design Personality — Good Grief & Co.

## Brand
Good Grief & Co. is a death-positive celebration-of-life planning studio in New Orleans. They help people plan their own funerals, living wakes, and "death parties" while they're still alive. Equal parts tender and irreverent — they believe grief deserves better than beige carpet and organ music.

## Users
- **The Life-of-the-Party Planner**: 50–70, knows what songs they want, strong opinions. Wants a send-off that feels like them.
- **The Loving Organizer**: 35–55, planning for a parent or processing loss. Wants guidance, not platitudes.

## Aesthetic
Editorial maximalism meets New Orleans warmth. Saturated jewel tones (amethyst, emerald, deep gold) against matte black, with hot magenta pops. The space is a converted shotgun house with velvet furniture, neon signs, cocktail bar, and Polaroid wall. Typography is bold and condensed like a fashion magazine covering mortality — sharp condensed sans (Oswald) for headlines, humanist sans (Source Sans 3) for body.

## Voice
Warm, witty, direct. Like a wise friend who brings champagne to hard conversations. Never morbid, somber, clinical, or preachy. Says "died" not "passed." Humor punches up, never down. Celebratory confidence that normalizes death planning.

## Quality Criteria
- **Color**: Jewel tones must feel rich and saturated, never muddy. Black should feel velvet-luxe, not gothic.
- **Typography**: Headlines should command attention like magazine covers. Body text must stay warm and readable.
- **Imagery**: Warm, intimate, candid. Golden hour and neon glow. Never clinical, never stock-photo sterile.
- **Tone**: Every piece of copy should feel like it could be said over a cocktail — honest, warm, maybe a little funny.
- **Layout**: Bold editorial grids with generous whitespace. Sections should breathe. Nothing cramped or cluttered.
- **Accessibility**: Death planning is for everyone. WCAG AA minimum. High contrast on all text.
