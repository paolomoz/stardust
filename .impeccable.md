# Design Personality

## Design Context

### Users
Senior-ish developers who have already tried three agent frameworks this quarter and are tired of servers, keys, and approvals. They land on SLICC while skimming between tabs — ten seconds to decide whether this is serious or another toy. They read fast, scroll faster, and will forgive ugly before they forgive generic.

**Job to be done:** decide in under a minute whether SLICC is a real tool worth installing, then get to the install command without being sold at.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **deadpan, mechanical, mischievous.**

- **Deadpan** — the brand does not sell. It states. Jokes are delivered without a smile.
- **Mechanical** — the product is a tool, not a platform. Copy and UI should feel like they were written by someone who builds things, not someone who names things.
- **Mischievous** — the ice-cream-meets-terminal vocabulary is load-bearing. "Self-licking ice cream cone", "Under the waffle hood", "chmod +x everything" are the brand, not decoration. One mischief per screen.

Emotional target: **impressed & intrigued.** Not delighted, not reassured. The visitor should think "wait, that's actually interesting" and want to read the next section.

### Aesthetic Direction

**References — pull FROM these:**
- **itch.io / Are.na** — scrappy, human, unpolished-on-purpose. Rough edges are a feature.
- **Raycast / Arc** — dense craft, playful micro-details, high attention to typography and motion. Serious product, plays in the margins.

The intersection is the target: Raycast's craftsmanship + itch.io's refusal to be slick. A tool that looks hand-built by someone who cares, not stamped out by a design system.

**Anti-references — explicitly avoid:**
- **Enterprise SaaS** — no stock photography, no "empower", no pastel gradients, no "diverse team at laptop" imagery.
- **Crypto / AI hype** — no cyan-on-black, no glow borders, no purple-blue gradients, no "revolutionary."
- **Corporate developer portal** — no cards-in-cards, no generic sidebar-docs aesthetic, no Inter-on-everything.
- **Toy / cartoon** — no mascots, no bouncy animations, no Comic-Sans energy. The playfulness is in the copy, not the shapes.

**Theme:** dark-primary (Cone Black #0C0C0C) with cream sections (#FAF9F7) for rhythm. Dark is the default surface; cream is the relief. This is not negotiable — it's extracted from sliccy.com and it's what the product already looks like.

**Typography:** the extracted fonts (Space Grotesk display/body, Space Mono for CTAs and eyebrows, Permanent Marker for rare scrawl accents) are on the `reflex_fonts_to_reject` list from the impeccable skill. They stay anyway — this is the brand's actual typeface, not a model default. Document this deviation in every downstream design so later passes know the deviation is intentional.

### Design Principles

1. **Earn every joke.** One mischief per screen, then shut up. If a dessert metaphor appears in the headline, the subhead is straight.
2. **Mechanical copy beats marketing copy.** "Your browser sessions, its hands" > "Empowering your workflow". Explain the machine, skip the benefit paragraph.
3. **Dark is the default; cream is a reward.** Alternate sections create rhythm — don't let five dark sections stack in a row, don't brighten to cream for no reason.
4. **Craft in the margins.** Typographic details, CTA weight choices, and motion timing are where the brand lives. The big shapes are simple on purpose.
5. **No AI tells.** No gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no icon-on-every-heading, no rounded-card grids. If a section pattern looks like it came out of a template, rebuild it.
6. **Ship it, iterate.** No hard WCAG tier set yet — aim for AA contrast and respect `prefers-reduced-motion` as a baseline, but don't let accessibility polish block a first pass.

### Rules to Remember

- Primary CTA is Cherry (#E0244F) filled + Space Mono label. Secondary is a ghost button with a hairline border. Never invert these.
- Body copy sits at ~0.85 opacity on dark, full opacity on cream. Recurring brand trait — preserve it.
- Strike-through rewrite ("An [ice cream] AI agent…") is a signature motif. Use it once per page, in the hero only.
- Permanent Marker is reserved for scrawls and arrows. Never for body, CTAs, or section headers.
- Tool-logo wall (GitHub, Figma, Slack, …) is a recurring "works with everything" signal. Monochrome only — never colored logos.
