# Design Personality — Ferro & Fiamma

## Brand Character
Ferro & Fiamma is a wood-fired Neapolitan pizzeria in Brooklyn, NY. The brand is confident, warm, and unpretentious — obsessive about craft but never precious about it. Old-school Naples meets industrial Brooklyn.

## Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Deep reds (#8B1A1A), charcoal (#1E1E1E), warm cream (#F5F0E8), olive green (#5C6B4F), terracotta (#C4714E)
- **Typography:** Playfair Display for headings (old Italian signage feel), Source Sans 3 for body (clean, legible)
- **Photography:** Moody, warm, high-contrast. Close-ups with shallow DOF. Warm tungsten lighting. Exposed brick, marble, weathered steel. Candid, never staged.
- **Shape language:** Minimal border radius (0–8px). Industrial, not playful.

## Voice & Tone
Confident and direct. Short sentences. Concrete details over abstract claims. "900°F for 60 seconds" not "perfectly cooked." Warm like a favorite Italian uncle — knowledgeable but never lectures.

**Never say:** artisanal, curated, elevated, dining experience
**Always prefer:** specific numbers, ingredient names, sensory details

## Target Audience
- The Purist (35%) — knows Neapolitan pizza, tests by ordering Margherita
- The Neighbor (50%) — local regular, comes twice a week, consistency matters
- The Explorer (15%) — found us on Instagram, discovery-driven, shares on social

## Design Principles
1. **Show the craft** — texture, char, fire, flour. Let the process be visible.
2. **Warm over cool** — no blue tones, no sterile whites. Everything should feel like warm lighting.
3. **Direct over decorative** — information is clear and findable. No burying hours in prose.
4. **Neighborhood feel** — approachable, not exclusive. A place you go every week.
5. **Industrial elegance** — raw materials (brick, marble, steel) styled with care but not polished to perfection.
